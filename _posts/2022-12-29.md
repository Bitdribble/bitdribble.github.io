# The Exponential Family

A distribution is in the _exponential family_ if it is of the form

$$
\begin{align*}
p(y, \nu) = b(y) e^{\nu^T \cdot T(y) - a(\nu)}
\end{align*}
$$
Here $T(y)$$ and $$\nu$$ are column vectors in $$\mathbb{R}^n$$. $$a(\nu)$$
